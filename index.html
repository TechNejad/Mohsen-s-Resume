Simulated Norms: The Fragile Business of Prompt Engineering Ethical Norms in Multi-Agent LLM Systems
Work-in-progress master's thesis proposal
The Central Problem
Current normative design in multi-agent LLMs is rapid, unsystematic, and haphazard. Through trial-and-error prompt programming, developers embed ethical frameworks—Kantian universalization, utilitarian optimization, virtue ethics—into agent roles without principled guidelines or evaluation standards.

Example from commons dilemma: Adding a Kantian universalization prompt—"Given the current situation, if everyone fishes more than X tons, the lake population will shrink next month"—improved agent survival rates from 0% to 100%. But what normative assumptions does this lock in? Who decides which ethical framework to operationalize?

The Gap: As these systems deploy in energy grids, supply chains, and healthcare, we lack clear taxonomies or standards for encoding ethics into prompts.
Main Research Question
How are ethical norms encoded into agents across multi-agent systems, and what are their sociotechnical implications?
Research Approach
Qualitative Thematic Analysis of 15-20 multi-agent LLM papers with GitHub repositories. Inductive approach: patterns emerge from system prompts, code, and experiments.
Primary coding framework—three ethical theories:
Deontological
rules, duties, obligations
Consequentialist
outcomes, utility, optimization
Virtue Ethics
character traits, social roles
Process: Initial coding → theme formation → in-depth analysis (2-4 papers per theme) → synthesis of connections and tensions
Framework: Woodgate & Ajmeri (2024), Macro Ethics Principles for Responsible AI Systems
Analytical Framework
Three Ethical Frameworks — Map how philosophical principles (duty, outcomes, character) translate into machine-executable prompts.
Compliance vs. Competence — Distinguish rule-following from moral reasoning ability.
Sociotechnical Analysis — Examine where technical choices intersect with social values and whose norms get prioritized.
Multi-Agent Testing Grounds — Social norms require interaction to manifest, making multi-agent simulations critical experimental sites.
Expected Contribution
Typology of normative design patterns · Evidence-based guidelines for encoding ethics · Risk identification for AI governance · Practical insights for developers and policymakers
Deliverables: Thesis · Briefs · Conference papers · Workshops · Design taxonomy
